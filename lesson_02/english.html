A bit about me

My name is Anastasiia, I am 28 years old, and by profession — a chemist. I was born in a small village in the Odesa region, but since my university days, I have lived in Odesa, which I immensely enjoy for its unique charm and proximity to the sea. This city has become my home.

My professional path

I worked for more than 3 years in the pharmaceutical company "Interchem". There, I was involved in:

  • Quality control of products
  • Chemical compound analysis
  • Participation in the development of new drugs

But in 2021, I went on maternity leave, and my life changed.

Finding Yourself in IT:

When I went on maternity leave, I became the mother of a wonderful girl and fully immersed myself in her upbringing. However, over time, I realized that working shifts and nights no longer suited my new lifestyle. This prompted me to seek remote work and reconsider my career plans.

I began exploring various job directions, taking online tests to understand what truly interests me. All tests pointed to one thing — front-end development. After that, I started actively searching for information, looking for educational resources, and by chance, I came across the YouTube channel "Freelancer LifeStyle". The videos from this channel captivated me so much that I watched them all in a short time and became literally passionate about mastering this profession.

My steps in learning

Since then, my journey into front-end development began:

  1. I completed several free lessons on YouTube.
  2. Finished front-end development courses.
  3. Comparing the training, I can say that the free lessons on YouTube were even more useful than the paid courses.

Unfortunately, the war has made adjustments to my life, and now my family and I are temporarily living in Ireland. My daughter has grown up, and I now have more time to achieve my professional goals.

That’s why I am currently taking this course — I want to continue to develop and become a specialist in the field of front-end development.

My skills and technologies

My main skills include:

  1. HTML (HyperText Markup Language)
    • Creation of the basic structure of web pages.
    • Organizing and formatting content such as:
      • Text and headings.
      • Images, videos, and other multimedia.
      • Links and lists.
    • Ensuring semantic markup for accessibility and SEO.
  2. CSS (Cascading Style Sheets)
    • Styling page elements with colors, fonts, and layouts:
      • Applying margins, padding, and borders.
      • Implementing custom fonts and typography styles.
    • Responsive design using media queries:
      • Adapting the layout for mobile, tablet, and desktop screens.
      • Creating fluid and flexible layouts with percentages and units like rem, vw, etc.
    • Animations and transitions for a dynamic user experience.
  3. Sass/SCSS (Syntactically Awesome Stylesheets)
    • Organizing styles with features like:
      • Variables for managing colors, fonts, and dimensions.
      • Nesting selectors for a cleaner structure.
    • Use of mixins and functions:
      • Reusable style blocks for consistent design.
      • Calculations and dynamic values within CSS.
    • Splitting code into partials and importing them for better maintainability.
  4. BEM (Block Element Modifier)
    • Code structuring methodology:
      • Breaking down the user interface into reusable blocks.
      • Defining elements within blocks for clear relationships.
    • Use of modifiers:
      • Easily changing block or element appearance through classes.
      • Managing multiple states (e.g., active/inactive, visible/hidden) cleanly.
    • Enhancing code readability and maintainability in large projects.
  5. Basic knowledge of JavaScript
    • Client-side programming:
      • Manipulating the DOM to update content dynamically.
      • Handling events like clicks, form submissions, and keystrokes.
    • Basic form validation
    • Creating interactive features:
      • Dropdowns, modals, and image sliders.
      • Implementing simple animations and transitions.
